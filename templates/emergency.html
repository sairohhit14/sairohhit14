Emergency First Aid Guidelines

Signs and Symptoms:
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Pain radiating to arm, shoulder, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
Immediate Actions:
  1. Call 911 immediately
  2. Have the person sit or lie down in a comfortable position
  3. Loosen any tight clothing
  4. If the person is not allergic to aspirin and has no condition that would make it unsafe, give them one aspirin to chew (if advised by emergency services)
  5. Stay with the person until emergency services arrive

Remember FAST:
  • Face: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
  • Arms: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
  • Speech: Ask the person to repeat a simple phrase. Is speech slurred or strange?
  • Time: If you see any of these signs, call 911 immediately.
Immediate Actions:
  1. Call 911 immediately
  2. Note the time when symptoms first appeared
  3. Do not give the person anything to eat or drink
  4. Have the person lie down with their head slightly elevated
  5. Stay with the person until emergency services arrive

Immediate Actions:
  1. Call 911 for serious bleeding
  2. Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth, bandage, or piece of clothing
  3. If possible, raise the wounded area above the level of the heart
  4. Continue to apply pressure until emergency services arrive
  5. If blood soaks through, add more layers without removing the first layer
Do not apply a tourniquet unless you have proper training.

For Conscious Adults and Children:
  1. Stand behind the person and slightly to one side
  2. Support their chest with one hand and lean them forward
  3. Give up to 5 sharp blows between their shoulder blades with the heel of your hand
  4. If the obstruction doesn't clear, stand behind them and place your arms around their waist
  5. Place your clenched fist just above their belly button, thumb-side in
  6. Grasp your fist with your other hand and pull sharply inward and upward
  7. Repeat up to 5 times (abdominal thrusts)
  8. If the obstruction still doesn't clear, continue alternating between 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts
  9. Call 911 if the obstruction doesn't clear

Emergency Telemedicine

Need urgent medical advice but not an ambulance? Connect with a doctor immediately via video consultation.

Doctors Available Now

Current wait time: ~2 minutes

When to use Emergency Telemedicine:
  • Minor injuries and illnesses
  • Medication questions
  • Non-life-threatening situations
  • When you're unsure if you need to go to the ER
Do NOT use for:
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Signs of stroke
  • Unconsciousness
  • Any life-threatening emergency

Call 911 for these situations
